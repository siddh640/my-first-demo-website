Class Policies

Guidelines to ensure a productive learning environment

  • Regular Attendance: Students are expected to attend at least 85% of scheduled classes to be eligible for certification.
  • Punctuality: Classes start on time. Students arriving more than 15 minutes late may be marked as absent for that session.
  • Absence Notification: Students must notify the administration office or their teacher in advance of any planned absences.
  • Make-up Classes: Up to three make-up sessions can be arranged per term for excused absences.
  • Consecutive Absences: Students absent for more than two consecutive weeks without notification may be withdrawn from the course.

  • Submission Deadlines: All assignments must be submitted by the due date. Late submissions may result in grade reduction.
  • Extension Requests: Extensions may be granted for valid reasons if requested at least 48 hours before the deadline.
  • Academic Integrity: Plagiarism and cheating are strictly prohibited and may result in course failure.
  • Examinations: Students must achieve a minimum score of 70% to pass course examinations.
  • Missed Exams: Students who miss an exam must provide documentation for their absence to be eligible for a make-up exam.

  • English Only: All communication in the classroom must be in English to maximize learning opportunities.
  • Respect: Students must show respect to peers and teachers. Disruptive behavior will not be tolerated.
  • Participation: Active participation in classroom activities is expected and contributes to the final grade.
  • Electronic Devices: Mobile phones must be silenced during class. Devices may only be used for educational purposes as directed by the teacher.
  • Food & Drinks: Only water is permitted in classrooms. Food and other beverages should be consumed in designated areas during breaks.

  • Grading Scale:
    • A (90-100%): Excellent
    • B (80-89%): Very Good
    • C (70-79%): Satisfactory
    • D (60-69%): Needs Improvement
    • F (Below 60%): Unsatisfactory
  • Grade Composition:
    • Attendance & Participation: 20%
    • Assignments & Homework: 30%
    • Mid-term Assessment: 20%
    • Final Examination: 30%
  • Level Advancement: Students must achieve a minimum overall grade of C (70%) to advance to the next level.
  • Grade Appeals: Students may appeal grades within one week of receiving them by submitting a written request to the academic director.

  • Library Usage: Students have access to the institute library during operating hours. Materials must be returned by the due date.
  • Computer Lab: The computer lab is available for educational purposes only. Food and drinks are strictly prohibited.
  • Learning Materials: Students are responsible for bringing required textbooks and materials to every class.
  • Property Care: Students must treat institute property with care. Damage to property may result in fines.
  • Study Rooms: Study rooms can be reserved up to one week in advance for group study sessions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if I miss a class?

If you miss a class, you're responsible for getting notes and assignments from classmates. For excused absences, you may be eligible for make-up sessions.

How can I request an extension?

Email your teacher at least 48 hours before the deadline with a valid reason for your extension request.

What if I need to withdraw from a course?

Course withdrawals must be submitted in writing to the administration office. Refunds depend on the timing of withdrawal as outlined in the tuition policy.
